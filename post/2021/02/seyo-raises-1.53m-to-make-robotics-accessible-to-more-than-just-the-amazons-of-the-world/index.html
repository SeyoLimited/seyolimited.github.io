Seyo raises £1.53m to make robotics accessible to more than just the Amazons of the world

by Yassir Al-Refaie | February 1, 2021 | Update

Logistics heavy companies understand the value of robots in their warehouses, a need that continues to grow. Many startups have emerged to provide these robots. But if you’re not Amazon or a Walmart, financing and implementing robotics automation is no walk in the park. As a result, only 0.2% of warehouses have implemented any form of robotic automation.

Seyo is filling this gap in the market by providing the first platform that allows companies to design their robotic warehouses, without needing advanced engineering skills. Their new approach to robotic fleet management makes it possible to control robot fleets in warehouses through an interoperability system between robots. Robots no longer operate in isolated environments and can communicate with each other, regardless of the manufacturer. Seyo is democratising access to robotic automation in mid-large warehouses, who now can use robotic automation from Goods-In all the way to Goods-Out all under a single platform.

“We are extremely excited to support Seyo” says Eva de Mol, Partner at CapitalT. “Not only is it an incredible team, they operate in a fast growing market, solving a key issue in that market.”

“We’ve been big fans of Yassir and Yanwen from the very beginning and we’re thrilled to see them raise this round to take the Seyo vision to the next level. This is a huge problem and the Seyo team have built a category-winning solution. We’re delighted to be investing again and supporting the next stage of the journey.” Jonny Clifford, General Manager, Entrepreneur First London

The foundation of Seyo were built by Yanwen Chan, the CTO of the company, during her 2 PhDs at INRIA and ECNU. There she focused on optimising distributed autonomous systems and built the fundamental algorithm that allows robots to self-orchestrate safely with each other even if they lose connection to central networks. Yanwen met Yassir Al-Refaie, the CEO of Seyo, at Entrepreneur First in 2019 in the talent investor’s 11th cohort, and they set to build what is now Seyo. Yassir’s background is in Electronics. His experience working with several Japanese companies trying to emulate the Amazon warehouse model and seeing first-hand the issues limiting mobile robotics adoption made him the perfect business partner for Yanwen’s technology. Quirino Zagarese, the Chief Architect brought his PhD in Service Computing for Ultra Large Scale Systems and his experienced software craftsmanship to the table. He has been crucial in productising and scaling Yanwen’s core technology.

Seyo will be using the money raised to develop the platform to support automation of all the main activities in a logistics warehouse, and to develop the simulation/ sandbox environment for customers to visualise potential automations in their warehouses before committing.
