Seyo secures Innovate UK grant to bring heterogenous robotic fleets to logistics warehousing

by Yassir Al-Refaie | May 25, 2021 | Update

Seyo, a platform built to improve adoption of robotics in Logistics Warehousing through interoperability has secured a £448k Innovate UK grant to deliver their core technology to a live warehouse environment in collaboration with Tu Pack a Third-Party Logistics company (3PL) based in Coventry.

E-commerce growth has supported the proliferation of 3PL services, however, it is difficult for these companies to stay competitive without embracing robotic automation. This problem has been exacerbated by the ongoing pandemic and the related social distancing measures, which necessarily reduce the ability to process parcels quickly and efficiently. Nonetheless, 3PL are struggling to migrate to robotic automation: this is not due to hardware availability or costs, given that solutions are available and can be affordably leased; the issue lies in fragmentation (i.e. robots from different manufacturers that do not talk to each other), which imposes expensive integrations (three to five times the cost of hardware) to those brave 3PL that take the leap. Ultimately, these solutions only address functional interoperability and do not provide warehouse-wide process optimisations, which makes them unsuitable for most 3PL scenarios. Seyo envisions SME 3PLs gradually transitioning to robotic automation using their platform, which will:

  • grow their profits by increasing the amount of processed orders within the time unit,
  • improve the quality of life for their employees, by allowing them to focus on value added tasks, whilst letting robots take care of the menial ones and
  • become more resilient to market fluctuations.

Seyo’s CEO, Yassir Al-Refaie says that “Supply chains are becoming more complex, with distribution and fulfillment centeres are the linchpin in that chain. Making robotics accessible to the broader market and not just concentrated with the Amazons of the world is hugely important in ensuring the global supply chain is robust, high-throughput and distributed. We are delighted that Innovate UK agree with us and recognise what we are trying to achieve.”

This marks a crucial milestone towards changing the way robotics are viewed and adopted, with a focus on solution-based platform design solving specific customer problems.
